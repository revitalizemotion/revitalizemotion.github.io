About me:

I believe that healthful activity improves our quality of life. I specialize in helping people overcome muscle imbalances that stem from injury, aging, or sedentary lifestyles. Having worked with everyone from those with severe arthritis to elite athletes, I teach my clients an applied philosophy: address weaknesses by harnessing strengths. This panoramic approach to wellness stems from my extensive background in the health sciences. After earning a B.S. in Nursing from University of Virginia, I worked as registered nurse for fifteen years. Then, in 2004, I decided to channel My desire to heal people into a career in personal training.

I develop creative, specific programs by drawing from a variety of personal training modalities. My proactive attitude toward education sets me apart as a perpetually growing resource for clients, students and colleagues. I have studied traditional personal training methods, a comprehensive Pilates curriculum, Ki-Hara resistance stretching, and most recently, Neurokinetic Therapy.

I live in Potomac, MD with my dog, Timba. In my free time, I enjoy reading, cooking, hiking, and spending time with my grown kids (when they let me). My latest interest is learning to ride horses again (especially dressage).
